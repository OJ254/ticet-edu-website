Data Protection Policy

Ticet EDU Limited (“Ticet,” “We,” “Our” “Us” “the company”) is an EdTech company focused on developing a platform where individuals can share educational and curriculum-approved content. This Data Protection Policy document is supported by other legal policies inclding our Terms of Use Policy and Privacy Policy. The policy may change, but whenever it does, we will indicate when it was last revised as hereunder.

We have created this Data Protection Policy to help you understand how Ticet handles and processes the data you share with us on our platfrom, https://ticet.edu, its subdomains, and any products or services offered by us (collectively called “the services”). We are committed to complying with all relevant, applicable legislation—local and international—touching on data protection.

Ticet recognizes that protecting individuals through lawful, legitimate, and responsible processing and using their personal data is a fundamental human right. We strive to ensure that we protect individuals' rights and that the data we collect and process is in accordance with current legislation and industry practice.

All our staff are required to comply with this policy, a breach of which could result in disciplinary action, including but not limited to suspension and dismissal.

If you are below 18 years of age, please share this data protection notice with your parent or guardian.

This data protection policy was last revised on 1st August 2024.


Definitions

Anonymization: Removing personally identifiable information from data sets so individuals cannot be identified.

(Personal Data) Breach: Unauthorized access, leakage, accidental loss of personal data.

Data Controller: A natural or legal person (like Ticet) who determines why and how personal data is processed.

Data Processor: A natural or legal person who processes personal data on behalf of the data controller.

Data Protection Impact Assessment [DPIA]: An exercise that an entity or an individual conducts to identify and mitigate against any data protection-related risks arising from a new project, which may affect your organisation or the individuals it engages with.

Data Subject: An identified or identifiable natural person who is the subject of personal data.

Encryption: Converting information or data into a code to prevent unauthorized access.

Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

Processing: Any operation or sets of operations performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means, such as (a) collection, recording, organisation, structuring; (b) storage, adaptation, or alteration; (c) retrieval, consultation or use; (d) disclosure by transmission, dissemination, or otherwise making available; or (e) alignment or combination, restriction, erasure or destruction.

Pseudonymization: Processing of personal data in a way that it cannot be attributed to a specific individual without additional information.

Regulator: Any local, state, national, or multinational agency, department, governmental body, regulatory authority, supervisory authority, board, or other public entity responsible for enforcing Data Protection and Privacy Laws.

What is the purpose of this policy?

The purpose of this policy is to guide Ticet staff on handling data that the organization collects, stores, transfers, analyzes, or processes in any other way. By ensuring compliance with data protection laws, this policy aims to protect the rights of both data subjects and staff while also supporting Ticet EDU's business goals. By prioritizing data protection, we enhance trust with our users and foster a secure learning environment, which is essential to maintaining our reputation and achieving long-term success.

Who does this policy apply to?

This policy shall apply to:

  1. Ticet employees, consultants, independent contractors, vendors, suppliers, and any other parties who handle and use personal information collected by us.
  2. All personal data processing that Ticet processes on behalf of other entities.
What are Ticet's data protection principles?

Ticet will ensure that any data that is processed is done so in accordance with the following principles:

  1. Right to Privacy: The data subject consents to the processing of their data. The consent shall be express, unequivocal and withdrawable at any time.
  2. Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: Data is processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner in relation to the data subject.
  3. Purpose limitation: Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes.
  4. Data minimization: Only data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed is collected. Where possible, data minimization techniques such as encryption, anonymization and pseudonymization are applied to protect the privacy of data subjects further and reduce the risks associated with data processing.
  5. Accuracy: Data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date, and reasonable steps will be taken to ensure that any personal data that is inaccurate, having regard to the purposes for which it is processed, is erased or rectified without delay.
  6. Storage limitation: Data is kept in a form that permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data is processed.
  7. Integrity and confidentiality: Data is processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorised or unlawful processing and accidental loss, destruction, or damage, using appropriate technical or organisational measures.
  8. Localization: Data is not transferred outside the country unless there is proof of adequate data protection safeguards or with the consent of the data subject.
What rights do data subjects have?

The Data Subject decides what specific information they want to access. This could include details about what personal data Ticet EDU holds on you, why it's being used, and who it has been shared with. The information to be accessed is more specifically described in (a) below.

  1. Subject Identifies What Data They Need: The Data Subject decides what specific information they want to access. This could include details about what personal data Ticet EDU holds on you, why it's being used, and who it has been shared with. The information to be accessed is more specifically described in (a) below.
  2. Download the Subject Access Request Form: Data Subject proceeds to download the [Subject Access Request Form] provided by Ticet EDU. They will fill in their details to help us identify the information they're requesting.
  3. Submit Request: The duly filled-in form is emailed to our Data Protection Officer at dpo@ticet.edu. The Subject attaches a copy of his/her ID for identity verification.
  4. Request Confirmation: After submission of the request, we send an acknowledgment email confirming that we've received the subject access request. This email is accompanied by timelines for a response and any other information that we may need to honor the subject's request.
  5. Await Our Response: All requests will be considered without undue delay and within fourteen (14) business days of receipt unless the request requires longer to process.
  1. Subject access: the right to request information about how personal data is being processed, including what personal data is being processed and the right to be allowed access to that data and to be provided with a copy of that data along with the right to obtain the following information:
    • the purpose of the processing
    • the categories of personal data
    • the recipients to whom data has been disclosed or which will be disclosed
    • the retention period
    • the right to lodge a complaint with the Regulator
    • the source of the information if not collected directly from the subject, and
    • the existence of any automated decision-making
  2. Rectification: the right to allow a data subject to rectify inaccurate personal data concerning them.
  3. Erasure: the right to request personal data be deleted under the following circumstances:
    • the data is no longer necessary in relation to the purpose for which it was collected, or
    • where consent is withdrawn, or
    • where there is no legal basis for the processing, or
    • there is a legal obligation to delete data
  4. Restriction of processing: the right to ask for certain processing to be restricted in any or all of the following circumstances:
    • if the accuracy of the personal data is being contested
    • if our processing is unlawful but the data subject does not want it erased
    • if the data is no longer needed for the purpose of the processing but it is required by the data subject for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims
    • if the data subject has objected to the processing, pending verification of that objection
  5. Data portability: the right to receive a copy of personal data which has been provided by the data subject and which is processed by automated means in a format which will allow the individual to transfer the data to another data controller. This would only apply if the company was processing the data using consent or based on a contract.
  6. Object to processing: the right to object to the processing of personal data relying on the legitimate interests processing condition unless Ticet can demonstrate compelling, legitimate grounds for the processing which override the interests of the data subject or for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.
How does Ticet handle data breaches?
  1. We will maintain a register of all data breaches.
  2. Ticet staff shall notify their line managers as soon as possible upon becoming aware of a personal data breach. The line managers shall, in turn, report the breach to the Data Protection Officer [DPO].
  3. The member of staff will record the breach noting to indicate if the breach is likely to result in personal injury or harm to a data subject.
  4. The individual will communicate the breach to the data subject within a reasonable time and take mitigating measures as appropriate without undue delay. Further, the company shall also notify the Data Protection Commissioner of the breach with the legally required timeline. The notification will describe:
    1. The nature of the personal data breach, including the categories and number of data subjects and data records concerned;
    2. The known and foreseeable adverse consequences of personal data breach; and
    3. The measures taken or proposed to be taken to mitigate and address the possible adverse impacts of the personal data breach.

How does Ticet ensure training and awareness about data protection?

Ticet EDU is dedicated to ensuring that all staff are well-informed about data protection practices and the importance of safeguarding personal data. To maintain high standards of data protection, the following measures are implemented:

  1. Regular Training: All employees will participate in mandatory data protection training at least once a year. This training will cover key aspects of data handling, legal obligations, and best practices. Refresher sessions may also be conducted as needed, especially following major updates to relevant laws, regulations or internal policies.
  2. External Trainers: To enhance the quality and depth of our training programs, Ticet EDU will periodically engage external data protection experts to conduct specialized training sessions. These sessions will provide advanced insights and up-to-date knowledge, ensuring our staff are equipped with the latest data protection skills.
  3. Mitigation Measures: Based on the findings of the DPIA, Ticet EDU will implement appropriate measures to mitigate identified risks. This may include enhancing security measures, adjusting data processing practices, or seeking additional guidance from data protection authorities.
  4. Data Protection Champions: Each department head is encouraged to appoint a Data Protection Champion. These champions will lead data protection efforts within their departments, organize training sessions, promote awareness, and ensure compliance with data protection protocols. They may also act as trainers of trainers who will be tasked with knowledge and capacity building within the organization.
  5. Ongoing Awareness: Ticet EDU will continually promote data protection awareness through internal communications, such as newsletters, bulletins, and workshops. This ongoing effort aims to keep data protection at the forefront of all activities, ensuring that staff remain vigilant and informed.

By implementing the above measures, Ticet EDU aims to foster a culture of data protection, ensuring that all staff are well-equipped to handle personal data responsibly and in compliance with applicable laws.

What is Ticet EDU's commitment to Data Protection Impact Assessment [DPIA]?

The Data Protection Act, 2019 (Kenya) requires organisations to undertake Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) where a data processing activity creates high risk to the rights and freedoms of a data subject.

Ticet EDU is committed to safeguarding the personal data of all individuals and ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws. To proactively manage and mitigate privacy risks, Ticet EDU will conduct DPIAs in the following situations as well as any other that are recommended by the country's data protection law.

  1. High-Risk Processing Activities: A DPIA will be conducted for any new or existing processing activities that may pose a high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects. This includes, but is not limited to, the processing of large volumes of sensitive personal data, implementing new technologies, or introducing significant changes to existing processes.
  2. Assessment Process: The DPIA process will involve identifying and evaluating the potential risks associated with the data processing activity, considering the nature, scope, context, and purposes of the processing. It will also involve consulting relevant stakeholders, including data subjects and external experts, where necessary.
  3. Mitigation Measures: Based on the findings of the DPIA, Ticet EDU will implement appropriate measures to mitigate identified risks. This may include enhancing security measures, adjusting data processing practices, or seeking additional guidance from data protection authorities.
  4. Documentation and Review: All DPIAs conducted by Ticet EDU will be thoroughly documented, including the rationale for conducting the assessment, the risks identified, and the measures taken to address those risks. DPIAs will be reviewed regularly to ensure ongoing compliance and relevance as part of our commitment to continuous improvement.

By integrating DPIAs into our data protection strategy, Ticet EDU ensures that potential risks to personal data are identified, assessed, and managed effectively, safeguarding the privacy and rights of our data subjects.

How is data handled upon death or account deletion?

Ticet EDU is committed to handling personal data with the utmost care and in accordance with the wishes of the data subject or legal requirements. In the event of account deletion or the death of a user, the following guidelines will be observed:

  1. Account Deletion: When a user requests the deletion of their account, all personal data associated with the account will be securely and permanently erased from our systems. This process will be completed in compliance with applicable legal requirements and within a reasonable timeframe, ensuring that no residual data remains unless required by law or for legitimate business purposes, such as dispute resolution.
  2. Handling Data upon Death: In the event of a user's death, Ticet EDU will act in accordance with the data subject's expressed wishes, if available, or as required by law. If the deceased user's next of kin or legal representative provides sufficient proof of death and requests the deletion of the user's account, we will ensure that all personal data is securely and permanently erased. Alternatively, data may be retained if legally required or if necessary for the resolution of any ongoing matters.
  3. Compliance and Documentation: All actions taken concerning the deletion of accounts and personal data, whether due to user request or death, will be thoroughly documented to ensure compliance with data protection regulations. Ticet EDU will also review and update these procedures regularly to align with legal developments and best practices.
How is this policy monitored and reviewed?

Ticet EDU is committed to regularly monitoring, reviewing, and updating this data protection policy at least once every year to ensure it remains effective and aligned with current legal requirements, best practices, and the following aims and considerations.

We recognize that data protection is an evolving field, and we are dedicated to continuous improvement in our practices. Feedback from data subjects, staff, suppliers, third parties and external stakeholders, as well as developments in data protection regulations, will be actively incorporated into this policy. This feedback may be facilitated through surveys, feedback forms, or focus group discussions, ensuring that the policy reflects the perspectives and needs of those it impacts. This approach ensures that our policy remains relevant and effective in addressing emerging data protection challenges.

Any significant changes will be communicated to all relevant parties, ensuring transparency and ongoing adherence to our data protection commitments.

What is Ticet's policy on data retention?

Ticet retains your data only as long as we reasonably require it and, in any event, only as long as you use our products and services. We will delete your data when you no longer use our products and services. We may retain logs of automatically collected information (for internal analytics), your email address, your tax information, communications with you, and your transactional information (for auditing, tax, and financial purposes). If we receive legal process about your use of our products and services, we will retain your data for as long as we, in good faith, believe it is necessary to comply with the legal process. Similarly, if we believe you have been involved in wrongdoing, we may preserve your data to defend or assert our rights.

How can you request the deletion of your personal data? What happens after you request deletion?

You may request the deletion of your personal data at any time. However, please note that this will prevent you from using some of our products or services for which the data was required. We will respond to deletion requests submitted via the app within one (1) month.

In regards to account deletion, you have a three-day grace period to cancel the deletion process by logging back into your account. If you do not cancel within this period, the deletion will become permanent and irreversible. If your account has active paid library subscriptions, the account will remain inaccessible to all users except active subscribers. These subscribers will continue to have access to your library until the end of their subscription period. The finalization of the deletion will occur at the expiry of the last active subscription. Once the deletion process is finalized, your personal data and account information will be permanently deleted and cannot be recovered. You will no longer be able to access any of our products or services that require the deleted data.
